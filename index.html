Learn the grammar

Form

The order of the in the Chinese "You" sentences are different from that in English. The prepostion always appears after the place. The place is the first element of the sentence.

Meaning

The meaning of Chinese "You" sentences is the same as the "There is/are" sentences in English: it refers to the existence or presence of someone/something in realtion to a place The keyword "有" is used to denote existence. It is different from the possesive "有" in sentences like "我有一本书" (I have a book).

Use

You will use "有" sentences rather than "在" sentences when you are not familar with the thing you are talking about. For example, if you are talking about your phone with your friends. And you want to say "My phone is on the table." You should use "在"。 But if you are talking about something else, and noticed a phone on the table, you shall use "有"。
